## Track 1 — Legal (the only route that can actually change the finding)

**Priority is time. Act before anything else.**

1. **Confirm representation.** Mr Gupta's former firm reportedly came off the record on 27 Jan 2026.
   First step: confirm whether he currently has solicitors. If not, engaging counsel is the single
   most urgent task — nothing below can proceed properly without them.

2. **Check the appeal deadline immediately.** When a lower court refuses permission, an appellant
   normally has a short window (typically 21 days from the decision) to renew the application for
   permission directly to the **Court of Appeal**. Given the refusal was in Feb 2026, that window may
   have closed. If so, an **application for an extension of time** is required, which needs a credible
   explanation for the delay. A solicitor must verify the exact date and status — treat this as the
   top action item.

3. **Engage commercial-litigation / appeals counsel.** Look for KCs/firms experienced in High Court
   commercial fraud appeals. I can help you build a shortlist and an outreach note (see Track 3).

4. **Define realistic appeal grounds.** An appeal is not a retrial. It must identify errors of law or
   procedural unfairness, or genuinely new evidence that could not have been produced at trial
   (high bar). Counsel decides what is arguable. Our job is to give them clean material to assess.

## Track 2 — Evidence brief for counsel (where I can add real value)

Goal: a single, factual, well-organised dossier counsel can read quickly — not advocacy, not spin.

1. **Chronology.** A dated timeline of the transactions, communications, and key events, sourced to
   documents.
2. **Document index.** Catalogue the WhatsApp messages, emails, contracts, and shipping/finance
   records already in the case, with a one-line description and date for each.
3. **Mr Gupta's account, point by point**, cross-referenced to supporting documents where they exist.
   (The trial finding turned partly on a lack of documentary support — so flag honestly where support
   exists and where it does not.)
4. **Candidate "new evidence" log.** Anything not before the trial court, with: what it is, why it
   wasn't available earlier, and what it would show. Counsel will judge admissibility.

## Track 3 — Media (narrow and accurate only)

1. **Hold position with one right-of-reply** (already drafted in the Response Pack): he disputes the
   judgment and is pursuing legal steps. Issue only if/when consistent with counsel's strategy.
2. **Correction requests** — only for specific, provable factual errors in a named article (wrong
   figure/date/entity). Not for disagreement with the court's conclusions.
3. **No mass outreach.** No bulk emails to publications, officials, or journalists; no statements
   asserting third parties' guilt. These create harassment, defamation, and contempt risk and would
   undermine any appeal.
4. **Counsel clears public statements first**, given live proceedings.
