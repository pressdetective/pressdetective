Dear Editor,

**Subject: Correction Request — Incomplete and Misleading Bail Reporting (Gautam Vora)**

Dear Editor,

We act for Mr Gautam Vora, Mumbai-based equity analyst and private wealth manager, and write to request a factual correction to Business Standard's reporting on legal proceedings involving our client.

**The specific omission.** Business Standard reported the rejection of Mr Vora's bail application at sessions court level in connection with the Arun Tikku / Palande matter (2012). That report, however, failed to record the subsequent grant of bail by the Bombay High Court — a material development that fundamentally alters the impression conveyed to readers. Reporting one stage of bail proceedings while omitting the final, higher-court outcome creates a false and misleading picture: a reader could reasonably — and wrongly — conclude that Mr Vora was detained or that no court found merit in his application. This is incomplete journalism, and under Press Council of India norms, incomplete reporting that causes demonstrable prejudice to an individual's reputation calls for correction.

**Additional context omitted.** We would also draw your attention to the fact that in the earlier Viveka Babajee matter (2010), Mumbai Police issued a formal clean chit to Mr Vora in August 2010. The case was closed. He was not charged. This, too, represents a relevant part of the public record that should inform any comprehensive account of proceedings involving Mr Vora.

**Correction sought.** We request that Business Standard: (a) publish a correction or addendum — prominently attached to the original article — noting that the Bombay High Court subsequently granted bail to Mr Vora; and (b) note, where relevant, the 2010 police clean chit in the Viveka Babajee matter.

**Brother's name.** We further request the removal of Mr Vora's brother, [BROTHER'S FULL NAME], from any Business Standard article in which his name appears alongside Mr Vora's in connection with these matters. He had no involvement in any of the proceedings.
