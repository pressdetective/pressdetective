---

**THE PUBLICATION:**

Times of India Mumbai published an article under the headline "Two bizmen
chargesheeted for assault, Rs 1cr extortion bid in '22" (byline: S Ahmed Ali)
naming the complainant, Tarun Thadani, in connection with FIR No. 0654/2022
registered at Dadar Police Station, Mumbai.

---

**VIOLATIONS:**

**1. Violation of Norm 13 (Pre-publication inquiry):**
The article reproduces a chargesheet narrative without independently verifying:
- Whether the named accused was present at the alleged incident location
- Whether the "extortion" allegation is supported by any evidence
- The financial and criminal antecedents of the complainant (Abhishek
  Badriprasad Saraf), who has documented fraud disputes before the
  Anti-Corruption Bureau

**2. Violation of Norm 7 (Right of Reply):**
No journalist from Times of India or Times Group made any attempt to contact
Tarun Thadani or his legal counsel before publication for comment or right of
reply. The article presents a single-sided account from the chargesheet.

**3. Material factual errors in the published article:**
- The article implies Thadani was present at the alleged incident — he was not
- The article describes an "extortion" demand — the reverse is true; Saraf owed
  money to Thadani and filed the FIR after a dispute over that debt
- The article makes no mention of the complainant's own record of disputes,
  which is relevant context for any reasonable reader

---

**RELIEF SOUGHT:**

1. A published correction and right-of-reply in Times of India Mumbai
2. A direction to Times Group's editorial policy to require right-of-reply
   attempts in all chargesheet-based crime reports
3. A direction that chargesheet reports include a standard disclaimer that
   a chargesheet is not a finding of guilt
